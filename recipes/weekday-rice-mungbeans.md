# Weekday Rice & Mung Beans

This is a reimagined version of a Cantonese zongzi that is much faster to prepare. This version highlights the benefits of eating pulses like mung beans to reach macronutrient goals. <https://www.madewithlau.com/recipes/bamboo-sticky-rice>

## Ingredients

### Rice and mung beans

- **150g** split mung beans
- **150g** rice (Recommendation: Traditionally glutonous rice is used, but any rice will work)
- **485g** water (Recommendation: Replace with liquid from soaking dried shittake mushrooms if using) (Recommendation: You may adjust water amount based on prefered texture)
- **15g** soy sauce or 3g of salt *(optional)*
<!---->
- **80g** mushrooms (optional) (Recommendation: 10-15g Dried shiitake mushrooms soaked overnight is traditional, but any kind of fresh or dried mushroom works)
<!---->
- **80g** Chinese sausage (optional) (Recommendation: Other cured meats such as bacon works as well)
<!---->
- **15g** lard or butter (optional) (Recommendation: Any flavored oils and fats will work)

## Step 1 (Prep mung beans and rice)

Combine and wash **mung beans** and **rice** until rinsing liquid comes out clear. Combine mixture with **water** and **soy sauce** and add to rice cooker or pressure cooker.

## optional step (Prep the mushrooms)

Wash **mushrooms** and dice into small pieces. Add to **rice and mung bean** mixture.

## optional step (Prep the cured meats)

Dice **Chinese sausage** into small pieces. Add to **rice and mung bean** mixture.

## Step 2 (Cook mung beans and rice)

Cook **mixture** in electric pressure cooker for 15 minutes on high pressure or normal setting in a rice cooker. Natural release if using pressure cooker.

## Optional step (Add fats)

Mix **cooked rice and beans** with **lard or butter** before serving.
