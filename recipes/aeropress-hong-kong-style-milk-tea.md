# Aeropress Hong Kong-style Milk Tea

Use an Aeropress and techniques from the coffee world to make Hong Kong's iconic drink at home.

## Ingredients

### For the tea

- **6g** loose leaf black tea (🧑‍🍳: Any blend of black tea will work, but make sure it is at least 60-80% tea fannings, the very fine type of leaves found in tea bags.)
- **300g** boiling water (🧑‍🍳: Remember to account for liquid lost to evaporation and absorption with the tea leaves. Feel free to adjust the amount of water to change the tea's strength.)
<!---->
- **60g** evaporated milk (🧑‍🍳: Alternative milks work but each will provide different mouthfeel, color, and sweetness to the tea)
- **5g** condensed milk or sweetener *(optional)*
<!---->
<!---->
<!---->
## Instructions

## Step 1 — Boil the tea

Add **black tea** and **boiling water** to a pot. Bring tea to a boil then simmer at low heat for **10 minutes.** (⚠️: Use larger pot or be aware of the heat to prevent the tea from overflowing.)

## Step 2 — Prepare milk

Add **evaporated milk** into serving cup. Add **sweetener** if using.

## Step 3 — Extract the tea and blend with milk

Setup Aeropress with a single paper filter. Pour entire **brewed tea**, including leaves, into the chamber. (❓: This step is to maximize extraction and to recreate the traditional "silk stocking" method of making Hong Kong milk tea.)

Push the plunger through the chamber gently until all liquid is passed through.

## Step 4 — Serve tea

Stir **milk tea** and serve
